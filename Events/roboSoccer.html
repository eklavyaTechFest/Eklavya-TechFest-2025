Robo Soccer

Overview

Engage in a thrilling robotic soccer match where teams compete to score goals while adhering to strict rules and guidelines. Showcase your engineering skills and strategic gameplay on the field.

Rules and Guidelines

  • Team Composition:
    • Each team can have 1-2 robots on the field.
    • A team may consist of 2-4 members (including operators and developers).
    • Only one operator is allowed to control the robot at a time.

  • Field Setup:
    • The field will be _m x _m with a goalpost of __cm width.
    • The ball used will be a lightweight plastic or foam ball.
  • Match Rules:
    • Each match consists of two halves of 5 minutes each with a 1-minute break.
    • The game starts with a kick-off from the center.
    • A goal is valid only if the ball fully crosses the goal line.
    • If the ball goes out, it is placed at the nearest touchline and play resumes.
    • Robots must not push, block, or trap the ball for more than 5 seconds.
    • No human intervention is allowed once the match starts (except in case of malfunctions).
  • Fouls & Penalties:
    • Continuous blocking or aggressive contact is a foul.
    • Deliberate holding of the ball for too long is penalized.

  • Scoring & Winning:
    • Each goal counts as 1 point.
    • The team with the most goals at the end wins.
    • In case of a draw, a penalty shootout (3 shots per team) will decide the winner.
